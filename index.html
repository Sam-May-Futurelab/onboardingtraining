Still Not Sure This Is For You?

Here are the questions I get most often:

Do I need this if we're still small?

Nope. 10 is the perfect size to fix things before they break. It's cheaper, faster, and less political now than it will be later.

How do I know when it's time for L&D?

When you're answering the same onboarding questions twice. When new hires take longer than expected to contribute. When you're scaling but systems aren't.

What if we're fully remote?

Even better. Remote teams need crystal-clear processes and documentation. No more "just ask Sarah" or hoping new hires figure it out from Slack.

Isn't this what managers should do?

Great managers absolutely should. But most are learning management while doing their day job. I help them get the frameworks and confidence to do it well.

How fast can you move?

I work in startup time. Most engagements start with something usable in week one. Full systems typically take 2-4 weeks, not months.

What if this doesn't work for us?

Then we haven't built the right thing. I'll iterate until it fits your team, or we'll part ways professionally. No drawn-out engagements.
